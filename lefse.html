Norwegian Lefse

Description:

Lefse is a traditional Norwegian flatbread, cooked on a large, flat griddle

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds of potatoes, peeled
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • Salt
  • White sugar
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream

Steps:

  1. Cover the potatoes with water and cook until soft. Rinse and place into a bowl.
  2. Beat the butter, cream, salt, and sugar into the hot potatoes. Let it cool to room temperature.
  3. Stir flour into the mixture. Pull off pieces of dough and form some balls the size of a walnut. Lightly flour a pastry cloth and roll out lefse balls to 1/8 inch thickness.
  4. Cook on a hot (400 degrees) griddle until bubbles form and each side has browned. Place on a damp towel to cool. Cover until ready to serve.
